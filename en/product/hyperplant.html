HyperPlant

Interaction & Systems Designer
  • Year: 2024
  • Type: Master’s Thesis & Functional Prototype
  • Status: Validated functional prototype

Interaction design · Platform architecture · Hardware-Software integration · Behavioral testing · UX research

A functional hybrid platform that translates real-time environmental data into a persistent digital avatar interface. By giving plants a "digital voice," the system is designed to support user awareness, care behavior, and long-term engagement through gamified, data-driven storytelling.

Impact & Key Outcomes

  • End-to-End System Orchestration: Independently designed and built a 0–1 platform that bridges physical environmental inputs with a digital interaction layer, ensuring data continuity from raw sensing to user-facing feedback.
  • Behavioral Framework Validation: Applied the Octalysis Framework to analyze and validate user motivation, proving that gamified "Digital Twins" successfully foster long-term care behavior and emotional bonding.
  • Data-to-Affect Translation: Developed an "Avatar Happiness Index" that converts complex environmental variables into intuitive, emotionally resonant visual states, making invisible data points actionable.
  • Research & Concept Validation: The conceptual framework and systemic methodology were peer-reviewed and presented at leading conferences in China (2020) and Colombia (2021), validating the project’s theoretical innovation prior to final dissertation.

Overview

HyperPlant is a hybrid physical–digital platform designed to help users build an ongoing relationship with their environment through real-time feedback and digital representation.
By translating ambient conditions into a persistent digital avatar, the system transforms a passive biological entity into an active Digital Twin. This feedback loop strengthens the user's sense of responsibility and engagement through emotionally-driven data representation.

System Architecture & Logic

I architected the prototype to ensure the ambient conditions surrounding the plant—its "living vitals"—are accurately reflected in the digital experience. I independently handled the orchestration of the data pipeline, selecting and connecting sensors to monitor variables like air quality and moisture, then mapping those inputs to the avatar's behavioral logic.

The environmental data collected was translated into a living digital avatar whose behavior reflected the plant’s comfort and care conditions. This created a direct feedback loop between the user's local environment and the plant’s digital presence.

The Digital Experience & Evolution

The experience allows users to perceive plant well-being through progressive visual feedback. This reinforces the interaction loop between care actions and the plant's response, making invisible environmental shifts legible through a simple, emotionally resonant representation.

By monitoring these shifts over time, the system provides a historical view of the interaction, allowing for a deeper understanding of the plant's environmental needs and the user's care patterns.

Behavioral Validation & Testing

Beyond the technical build, the project focused on Behavioral Validation. To evaluate whether the platform fostered emotional engagement and care behavior, I ran a one-week prototype test and analyzed user motivation using the Octalysis framework.

Strategic Learnings

  • The Power of Representation: Giving a voice to the invisible (air quality, moisture) through an avatar drastically increases a user's sense of "Emotive Responsibility."
  • Informed Data Experience: The experience bridges rational data with emotional engagement, ensuring that technical integrity translates into long-term behavioral change.

Notes
