About

I am an interdisciplinary designer working at the intersection of systems, interaction, and emerging technologies. With a background in architecture and master’s degrees in interactive design and creative computing, I approach product design through a systemic perspective—connecting spatial reasoning, digital environments, and computational processes to shape clear and scalable user experiences.

I transitioned from architectural systems to hybrid digital–physical systems, working on projects in digital transformation, healthcare, subscription platforms, IoT-driven interaction, and blockchain-enabled storytelling. Across these contexts, my focus has been consistent: making complexity understandable, trustworthy, and human-centered.

My recent work explores decentralized interactions, data-informed narratives, and multi-state user flows, where thoughtful UX and strong systems thinking have a direct impact on transparency, trust, and decision-making. I enjoy designing environments where users can navigate technological complexity with clarity.

I am also interested in how nature and technology can inform more intuitive and meaningful forms of interaction. This perspective shapes my design philosophy and my approach to creating systems that feel coherent, human, and grounded—even within emerging or experimental contexts.
