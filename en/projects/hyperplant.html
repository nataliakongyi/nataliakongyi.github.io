HyperPlant

2024 — Thesis project · Master in Interactive Design (University of Buenos Aires)

Designer (Interaction & Systems) · Creative Technologist · Research-led Practitioner

bio-inspired design sustainability speculative design service design interaction design

Context

By 2050, almost three planets’ worth of resources would be required to sustain current lifestyles shaped by consumerism and unsustainable production patterns. This scenario is rooted in a longstanding cultural paradigm that separates humans from nature, promoting the illusion of environmental dominance and unlimited resources.

HyperPlant is an interactive system that explores the relationship between humans and plants through multisensory engagement. Designed for indoor spaces and tested in Lima, the project investigates how technology can foster empathy and environmental awareness by making interspecies exchanges perceptible. Developed as a functional prototype, it proposes a framework for human–plant connection based on air phytoremediation and the perception of reciprocal care.

Challenge

How can we reimagine our relationship with nature through a systemic and experience-driven approach?

HyperPlant is a one-to-one bonding system that translates environmental conditions—air quality, humidity, and temperature—into intuitive, real-time feedback. Embedded sensors collect and stream data to a digital platform, transforming everyday objects into responsive elements.

The project is grounded in three key concepts: bonding, smart spaces, and hybrid realities. The latter extends the physical presence of the plant into a virtual environment where a digital avatar mirrors its condition—adding emotional depth and narrative coherence to the interaction. This immersive strategy is complemented by gamification to encourage long-term engagement.

Outcome

Five plants were shortlisted to develop the avatars and introduce a sense of realism into the digital platform, strengthening engagement and attachment. The prototype follows a maker-oriented approach using a 3D-printed case, an Arduino board, and environmental sensors. Its gamified interface explores motivation while envisioning future possibilities for collective interaction.

During a one-week testing phase, the participant quickly developed an emotional connection with the plant mediated by its avatar. The user's diary revealed attachment, empathy, and growing awareness—confirming the potential of interactive media to strengthen interspecies bonds.

HyperPlant demonstrates how data, design, and narrative converge to create hybrid realities that bridge physical and digital experiences. It positions interaction design as a tool to cultivate awareness—not only of environmental conditions but of the affective ties that shape coexistence with other living systems.

Notes

Conceived and developed as a prototype exploring empathy, interaction, and environmental awareness through data, hybrid realities, and gamification. The learnings inform future product and service concepts and interaction models.
This is my thesis project for the Master in Interactive Design at the University of Buenos Aires. Watch my dissertation.
Previous versions of HyperPlant were presented at the Chinese Conference on Bio-inspired Design and Technology (China, 2020) and The International Image Festival (Colombia, 2021).
Tested in Lima, Peru, within a research-driven framework integrating descriptive and prescriptive design stages.
Tools and resources: Arduino, digital fabrication, HTML/CSS — combining environmental sensors and narrative systems with AI-assisted iteration.
