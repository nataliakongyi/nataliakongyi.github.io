Seniors Club

Co-founder & Strategy Designer
  • Year: 2025
  • Type: Independent Product Concept
  • Status: Strategy & Concept | Strategic design and service architecture

Service design · Business strategy · Stakeholder mapping · UX research · Product vision

Platform concept connecting older adults with curated activities, social networks, and wellbeing services through a subscription-based digital and physical experience.

Impact & Key Outcomes

I defined the conceptual platform structure and service architecture, translating research insights into an initial interaction model and lifecycle framework. This included:

  • Service Ecosystem Architecture: Defined a 0–1 platform structure that translates fragmented care needs into a coordinated multi-stakeholder interaction model.
  • Lifecycle & Journey Mapping: Structured the end-to-end user experience, from emotional onboarding and trust-building to long-term engagement rituals and well-being tracking.
  • Value-Exchange Logic: Established the participation incentives for the "care network" (seniors, families, and partners) to ensure a sustainable social-impact business model.
  • Qualitative Research: Conducted field research and unstructured interviews to map the "invisible labor" of care, identifying key friction points in existing fragmented services.

Overview

Seniors Club is a hybrid service and digital platform concept designed to foster autonomy and social connectivity for older adults. By synchronizing curated physical activities with a unified coordination layer, the system addresses the "loneliness epidemic" in aging populations. The project connects families and providers into a single, supportive ecosystem that reduces the cognitive load of care for families while empowering seniors.

Service Strategy & User Journey

The strategic foundation of the project lies in the synchronization of the digital interface with the physical service lifecycle. I designed the stages of the journey to ensure technology acts as a silent enabler of human connection, focusing on the transition from digital discovery to real-world experience.

User Journey Overview

Discovery

Curiosity and emotional resonance — “This feels like something for me (or for my dad).”

Gift or Subscription

Emotional motivation — “I want to gift something that feels human and useful.”

Welcome & Onboarding

Excitement about practical uses — easy onboarding and a physical welcome box.

Exploration & Booking

Empowerment and curiosity — friendly assistant, discover activities, and save favorites in one place.

Engagement & Well-being

Joy and sense of belonging — connect through experiences. Access discounts, wellness offers, and small daily rituals.

Reflection & Retention

Connection and gratitude — visualize milestones and share stories.

Strategic Learnings

  • Systemic Foundation: This project reinforced the necessity of designing participation incentives and "value loops" before defining interface solutions. In complex service design, the "product" is the relationship between actors.
  • Multi-Actor Alignment: High-impact social products require aligning the incentives of the entire care network. Success is measured by the well-being of the senior and the peace of mind provided to the family.

Notes

Entreprenurial Vision: Originally conceptualized as a venture to address the fragmentation of the "Silver Economy" in Latin America.
Context: Designed and researched in Lima, Peru, with a focus on cultural patterns of family-led care.
Ownership:As Co-founder, I led the entire strategic definition, including the business model canvas, stakeholder interviews, and service blueprinting.
