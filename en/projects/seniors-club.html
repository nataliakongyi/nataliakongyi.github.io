Seniors Club

2025 — Service design · Startup

Service Designer · UX Strategist

service design community design

Context

Seniors Club explores strategic design in active aging. Conceived as a subscription-based service, it connects seniors with cultural, recreational, and wellness activities through partnerships — promoting autonomy, connection, and purpose in later life.

Challenge

How can design foster independence and a sense of belonging in an aging society?

Many older adults experience isolation, while family members often lack the tools to help them stay engaged. Seniors Club proposes a dual experience model: one for active seniors who seek independence and community, and another for their close network, who want accessible ways to support them. The challenge lies in balancing usability with emotional resonance — creating an experience that empowers rather than assists, and framing autonomy as a shared value.

User Journey Overview

Discovery

Curiosity and emotional resonance — “This feels like something for me (or for my dad).”

Gift or Subscription

Emotional motivation — “I want to gift something that feels human and useful.”

Welcome & Onboarding

Excitement about practical uses — easy onboarding and a physical welcome box.

Exploration & Booking

Empowerment and curiosity — friendly assistant, discover activities, and save favorites in one place.

Engagement & Well-being

Joy and sense of belonging — connect through experiences. Access discounts, wellness offers, and small daily rituals.

Reflection & Retention

Connection and gratitude — visualize milestones and share stories.

Outcome

Seniors Club outlines a scalable subscription service with curated activities, member benefits, and an intuitive digital interface that bridges generations. It reframes aging through interaction, agency, and care — helping seniors remain active and connected.

Currently in development as a startup prototype, the project continues to evolve as a framework for community-centered health and well-being.

Notes

Conceived and developed as an independent service design concept.
Tools and resources: Service blueprinting, user journey mapping, Figma, prototyping, and content strategy.
Key concepts: active aging, social inclusion, community networks, and behavior-driven engagement.
