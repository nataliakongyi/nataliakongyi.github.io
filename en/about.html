About

I am an interdisciplinary designer and researcher working at the intersection of the built environment, technology, and interaction. Drawing from architectural thinking, interactive design, and creative computing, my work focuses on shaping how complex systems are experienced, understood, and engaged.

My practice has evolved from designing physical spaces to prototyping hybrid physical–digital systems across digital transformation, healthcare, subscription-based services, IoT-driven systems, and blockchain-enabled narratives. I translate complex structures into clear, meaningful experiences through hands-on, research-informed design.

I work by defining experience principles, interaction logics, and system-level frameworks, prototyping selectively to make decisions tangible. I design interfaces when needed, with a deliberately minimal, clarity-driven approach, particularly in data- and AI-informed contexts.

I am especially interested in how nature and technology can enable more situated and intuitive ways of living and interacting, making complexity perceptible, relational, and actionable.
