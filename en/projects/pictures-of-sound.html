Pictures of Sound

2025 — Data visualization and sound exploration · ELISAVA / UVic-UCC
digital culture data art interaction design

Context

Pictures of Sound explores how sound can be experienced visually through data. The project transforms unstructured sound information — frequency and amplitude — into generative visual landscapes that resemble abstract maps. Using real-time sound analysis, it draws continuous Brownian paths whose color and length respond to variations in the audio signal. The resulting images evoke topographies of sound, inviting reflection on how perception translates across senses and mediums.

Challenge

How can we reimagine the perception of sound beyond hearing?

The project explores how data visualization and generative art can make the intangible nature of sound perceptible, spatial, and emotional. It uses the microphone as input to capture frequencies and amplitudes, organizing them into ranges that define motion and color parameters — converting data into visual rhythm.

Outcome

Pictures of Sound presents an evolving generative composition that renders sound as dynamic drawings, blurring the boundaries between listening and seeing. Each visualization becomes a “portrait of sound” — unique to its moment and place of recording. Future iterations consider retrieving color palettes from each location and expanding the system into immersive environments (AR or physical installations), allowing users to step inside the visualization and experience sound as spatial form.
