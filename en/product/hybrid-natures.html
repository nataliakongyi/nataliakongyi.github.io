Hybrid Natures

Systems & Interaction Design
  • Year: 2025
  • Type: Master's Thesis & Ongoing Platform Development
  • Status: Deployed proof-of-concept platform prototype

Platform strategy · UX research · Platform and system architecture · Interaction design · Generative systems · Prototyping

A 0-to-1 platform that translates complex Amazonian environmental data into intuitive narrative interactions and generative artifacts. The project bridges the gap between abstract climate data and tangible civic participation through a scalable, multi-actor ecosystem.

Impact & Key Outcomes

  • Data-to-Narrative Orchestration: Architected a 0–1 platform that converts curated environmental datasets into progressive user journeys, making complex ecological shifts legible through storytelling.
  • Full-Stack Prototype Development: Independently built a functional web-based proof-of-concept. This included coding generative systems in p5.js and deploying a Web3 infrastructure to validate digital ownership (NFTs) as a participation mechanism.
  • Tiered Engagement Framework: Structured a scalable participation model that allows users to transition from passive discovery to active contribution and artifact ownership.
  • Systemic Scalability: Designed the logic for a distributed interface strategy, including narrative-driven physical hotspots (mockups) to extend engagement into public and cultural spaces.

Overview

Hybrid Natures is a participatory platform designed to bridge the gap between abstract climate data and personal engagement. By translating curated scientific data into generative artifacts, the system enables users to explore environmental causes, contribute to collective action, and build ongoing relationships with real-world ecosystems.

The project operates across multiple scales, connecting global environmental data with local territorial realities. This first edition was about the Amazon Rainforest. By mapping the relationship between deforestation hotspots in the Amazon and the distributed network of the platform, I defined a geographic strategy that ensures the system remains grounded in the specific socio-environmental needs of the region.

Technical Implementation: Data & Generative Logic

To ground the system in ecological reality, I distilled complex data about the Amazon Rainforest from scientific literature into structured datasets. I analyzed this data to construct a narrative based on progressive cognitive proximity.

Using these curated datasets, I developed a generative system that translates river discharge and deforestation variables into dynamic digital artifacts. The series of generative art pieces function as the primary interaction interface, designed to foster a connection between the user and the territory.

System Components & Scalability

The platform was conceived as a multi-actor ecosystem designed to scale. While the core digital prototype validates the data-to-interaction engine, the broader architecture includes a strategic roadmap for physical and collective expansion:

Core prototype components:

  • Four-Tier Participation Model: I designed a scalable framework that guides users from passive discovery to active contribution and digital ownership, establishing the foundation for an inclusive participation economy.
  • Governance Framework: Defined the structural logic for future collective decision-making, ensuring the platform can evolve into a self-sustaining community asset.
  • Narrative Hotspots: Beyond the web interface, the system includes a strategy for AR-enabled hotspots that link physical locations (using real rainforest footage) back to the digital platform.

The system architecture combines a central digital platform with a strategy for distributed physical access points. These physical elements were defined as part of the implementation roadmap, utilizing narrative-driven hotspots to extend environmental discovery into public and cultural spaces.

Within Hybrid Natures, these artifacts are not merely outputs; they function as participation interfaces that enable ownership and progressive engagement across the platform’s tiered model.

Notes

Deployed proof-of-concept platform prototype independently designed and developed, initially created as my Master’s thesis in Creative Computing (ELISAVA & UVic-UCC), and continued as an ongoing platform development project.
The project was later presented at the International Colloquium Extractivismos (Argentina, 2025), a research forum exploring socio-environmental systems and cultural infrastructures.
I led all stages of the project, including platform architecture, interaction design, data integration, and generative system development. This included designing the participation model, defining the token logic, and deploying the smart contract infrastructure supporting artifact ownership.
Generative artifacts were created using p5.js and real environmental data, and deployed through a Web3 infrastructure as part of the platform’s participation model.
Hybrid Natures serves as an active foundation for ongoing platform development and future deployment.
