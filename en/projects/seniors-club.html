Seniors Club

2025 — Service design · Startup
service design community design

Context

Seniors Club explores strategic design in active aging. Conceived as a subscription-based model, it connects seniors with cultural, recreational, and wellness activities through partnerships — promoting autonomy, connection, and purpose in later life.

Challenge

How can design foster independence and sense of belonging in an aging society?

Many older adults face isolation, while relatives and friends often lack the tools to help them stay engaged. Seniors Club proposes a dual experience model — one for active seniors seeking independence and community, and another for their close network, who want to help them find activities an spaces for interaction. The challenge lies in balancing usability and emotional resonance, creating an experience that empowers rather than assists, promoting autonomy.

User Journey Overview

Discovery

Curiosity and emotional resonance — “This feels like something for me (or for my dad).”

Gift or Subscription

Emotional motivation — “I want to gift something that feels human and useful.”

Welcome & Onboarding

Excitement about practical uses — easy onboarding and a physical welcome box.

Exploration & Booking

Empowerment and curiosity — friendly assistant, discover activities and save recipes in one place.

Engagement & Well-being

Joy and sense of belonging — connect through experiences. Enjoy discounts, wellness offers, and small daily rituals.

Reflection & Retention

Connection and gratitude — visualize milestones and share stories.

Outcome

Seniors Club outlines a scalable subscription service with curated activities, member discounts, and an intuitive digital interface that bridges generations. It reframes aging through interaction, care and benefits, to help them keep active.

Currently in development as a startup prototype, the project continues to evolve as a framework for community-centered health and happiness.
