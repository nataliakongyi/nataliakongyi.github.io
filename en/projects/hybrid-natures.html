Hybrid Natures

2024 — Final project · Master in Creative Computing (ELISAVA / UVic-UCC | SHIFTA)

Designer, Researcher & Creative Technologist

digital culture sustainability data art gamification interaction design

Context

Hybrid Natures began as my thesis project for the Master in Creative Computing at ELISAVA / UVic-UCC. It aims to raise awareness about environmental crises, from a systemic perspective, to promote individual and collective engagement.

By integrating spatial and digital layers, it connects people, communities, and ecosystems, using data as a catalyst — exploring how technology can foster new forms of empathy and engagement with endangered causes. The first edition is about the Amazon Rainforest.

Challenge

How can design and technology help us understand better the relationship between humans and nature from a systemic perspective?

Hybrid Natures explores this question with a hybrid system, merging a digital platform and decentralized elements to connect users with endangered territories and indigenous communities. The digital environment is complemented with physical supports that display visual and audiovisual media, creating portals to those ecosystems.

Outcome

The platform’s narrative unfolds the Amazon Rainforest progressively — first highlighting the relevance of each selected cause, then revealing its vulnerabilities, and finally establishing a closer connection to the viewer’s everyday life. This sequence is designed to build both an emotional and cognitive bridge, transforming awareness into active participation. At this stage, Hybrid Natures introduces a series of generative artworks in NFT format, following a tiered distribution model with variable donation percentages to nonprofit organizations engaged with the cause. All the art pieces were created using p5js and data from the Amazon Rainforest. They were minted, listed and showcased in Manifold.

The result is a hybrid (physical–digital) experience that merges data, imagery, and digital art within a decentralized and inclusive, decentralized framework inspired by Web3 principles. It integrates gamification strategies to foster collective, long-term engagement. Through the convergence of design, art, and technology, Hybrid Natures invites participants to rethink their relationship with nature — transforming the act of observing into one of belonging.

Notes

Conceived and developed independently as a working digital platform integrating research, design, and prototyping explorations. The current version represents an initial phase of implementation, with additional content planned for future development.
Originally created as an individual thesis project for the Master in Creative Computing at ELISAVA / UVic-UCC (through SHIFTA).
All stages — from concept and web architecture to data processing, content development, and generative artwork — were carried out independently as part of the research process.
The project’s website presents data visualizations, within an engaging narrative about the Amazon Rainforest, and a series of generative artworks later minted through Manifold, extending its exploration of decentralization and collective authorship.
Presented at the International Colloquium Extractivismos (Argentina, 2025).
Tools and resources: p5.js, HTML/CSS, and Flourish — integrating web development and generative systems with AI-assisted iteration.
