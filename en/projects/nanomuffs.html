Nanomuffs

2025 — Dystopian future exploration · ELISAVA / UVic-UCC
bio-inspired design speculative design

Context

Nanomuffs is a conceptual proposal that explores how biotechnology could reshape perception and human interaction. Set in a dystopian scenario where CRISPR-based genome editing has been widely adopted and normalized, some genetic conditions have been cured — yet new sensory alterations have emerged, raising complex ethical questions about this kind of technologies. In this context, younger generations experience hypersensitivity and struggle to cope with sounds, affecting their interactions and social skills. Nanomuffs imagines a wearable device for children with sound hypersensitivity, combining narrative and design to transform our ways of perceibing and interacting with others.

Challenge

How might inclusive design foster empathy and ease interaction in a world of amplified perception?

Nanomuffs is a wearable system that interprets the user's auditory comfort and translates it into facial expressions that support mutual understanding. The device connects biological, digital, and emotional dimensions of care, suggesting how future technologies could mediate sensory experience while promoting empathy rather than isolation.

Outcome

Nanomuffs proposes a speculative framework for technology in inclusive design, grounded in empathy, perception and sensory awareness. By merging biotechnology and design fiction, it questions the direction of current challenges toward dystopian futures, envisioning a wearable and an app for inclusion.

Acknowledgements

Conceived as a speculative exploration about biotechnology, empathy, and sensory design. Created within the framework of the Master in Creative Computing at ELISAVA / UVic-UCC.
